Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) have rapidly evolved over the last decade. 
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that enhances large language models (LLMs) by 
providing them with external, factual context before they generate a response. 
This reduces hallucinations and ensures answers are grounded in reality. 
Vector databases like ChromaDB are often used to store embeddings of documents so they can be 
quickly retrieved using similarity search (e.g., Cosine Similarity).
